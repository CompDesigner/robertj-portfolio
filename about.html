Where it started...

Growing up I played a lot of video games like every normal kid does until one day I thought about what hardware was inside my gaming console. What kind of hardware is used and how is it designed? This lead to the pursuit of my Computer Engineering degree at the University of Central Florida (UCF) and my passion for technology advancements in computer hardware and eventually robotics. I didn't feel the impacts of knowledge until I got further in my degree and took my first true engineering course, Digital Systems.

In that course I learned all about logic gate design and the way hardware is structured using Verilog. I got inspired to continue on with my learning until I reached my next breakthrough which was Embedded Systems. I was able to use serial communication protocols such as UART, I2C and SPI to communicate with a MSP430 microcontroller including a Booster Pack sensor board. After that course I felt it was time to apply already gained skills and practice learning more skills by getting involved into the engineering clubs at UCF.

Getting Involved!

It was around my sophomore year when I went to my first IEEE general body meeting. I felt right at home with fellow engineering students and was eager to jump into any of the projects and workshops they had going on. I started off with getting my IEEE membership and joining mainly the workshops first.

I attended a couple of workshops such as Verilog/HDL hardware designing, Arduino programming, and a paper speaker design workshop. After my sophomore year I took on becoming a Workshop Committee member and Marketing Committee member. I held a I2C/UART Interface workshop and marketed IEEE events as well as projects. During the same time I became the Hardware Sub-lead for the SouthEast Conference (SEC) Hardware Competition were I assisted in 3D modeling and building a robot.

My Last Year

At the end of my junior year I ran for the Project Director position in the new officers election for Biomedical Engineering Society (BMES) and I got elected as the new Project Director. It was stemmed from my search of robotic internships during the summer between my sophomore and junior year.

During my search I came to the realization of all the areas involving robotics and I focused my attention on medical robotics and technology. I also self taught myself computer vision using Python and OpenCV. After becoming the Project Director I got right to work by forming a project committee and planning out projects for the year. The project committee and I came up with two main projects at first then another main project later on in spring which was my last term. We also had one smaller project which was not as extensive as the main projects.

I took on the workshops for my last term along side assisting with the projects and driving them forward. During the time as Project Director I was working on my part for my teams senior design capstone project. In the end at the CREOL Optics and Photonics Senior Design Showcase my team was awarded first place for our project "ISPY" (Image Surveillance Protection Yielder).
